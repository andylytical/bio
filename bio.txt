Andy Loftus is a Senior Systems Administrator at the
National Center for Supercomputing Applications,
which roughly translates as technology wizard focused on
making computers do as much as possible so he doesn't have to.
He designed the backup software for the Blue Waters supercomputer
to ensure the safety of all data on it's dual 2 petabyte filesystems.
He is currently using Git and Puppet to automate system deployment for the
LSST project, an amazing new telescope high on a mountain top in Chile.
When he's not automating computers, he can be found
coaching volleyball, spending time with his family,
and being an amateur brewmaster.
